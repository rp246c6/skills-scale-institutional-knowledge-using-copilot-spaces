# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation suite. This README provides a high-level overview of our project management processes and serves as a central hub for navigating detailed process documents.

## Purpose

These documents define how OctoAcme runs projects, manages teams, communicates with stakeholders, and continuously improves our delivery processes. They are designed to support onboarding, provide consistent guidance across teams, and ensure alignment on shared practices.

## OctoAcme Project Management Overview

OctoAcme uses a lean, iterative project management approach that combines Agile planning principles with clear stage-gates for initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospectives. Our approach prioritizes customer value, psychological safety, clear ownership, and data-informed decisions.

### Key Elements

**Initiation**: Document value, stakeholders, and success criteria to establish a shared understanding of project goals and scope.

**Planning**: Define scope, milestones, responsibilities, and dependencies; create lightweight plans and release checklists with input from all key roles.

**Execution & Tracking**: Use short iterations with regular standups (daily), visible project boards, and weekly status updates. Maintain a project rhythm that enables rapid feedback and course correction.

**Risks & Communication**: Maintain a risk register with impact/likelihood assessments and mitigation plans. Define escalation paths and hold regular stakeholder syncs to ensure visibility and alignment.

**Release & Deployment**: Follow a release checklist with roll-back plans and post-release verification. Coordinate with all stakeholders to ensure smooth transitions to production.

**Retrospectives**: Run sprint and release retrospectives to capture learnings, celebrate wins, and identify action items. Assign owners and track improvements over time.

### Core Roles & Responsibilities

- **Project Manager (PM)**: Coordinates delivery, manages schedules, risks, and communications. Ensures alignment across teams and stakeholders.
- **Product Manager (PdM)**: Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures success through metrics and user research.
- **Developers**: Implement features collaboratively, participate in design and code reviews, and maintain quality standards through testing and documentation.
- **QA/Testing**: Validate quality and acceptance criteria through manual and automated testing.
- **Stakeholders**: Provide inputs, approvals, and strategic guidance to ensure alignment with organizational goals.

## Communication Cadence

All OctoAcme projects follow a consistent communication rhythm:

- **Daily**: Team standups (15 min) — focus on progress, blockers, and dependencies
- **Weekly**: PM + PdM sync and delivery team weekly sync to review progress and flagged risks
- **Monthly**: Stakeholder updates to share status, decisions, and strategic context
- **As-needed**: Ad-hoc escalations and incident response communication

## How to Contribute

We encourage continuous improvement of these processes. If you want to propose updates or additions:

1. **Identify the change**: Determine which document needs updating or if you're proposing a new process area
2. **Open an issue**: Create a GitHub issue with the label `documentation` and describe:
   - Which document(s) are affected
   - Summary of the proposed change
   - Why the change is needed
   - Suggested content or links
3. **Create a pull request**: Reference the issue and include:
   - Clear commit message with issue reference (e.g., "Closes #X")
   - Updated document(s) with your proposed changes
   - Context on how the change aligns with existing processes
4. **Review & iterate**: Tag relevant reviewers (PM, Product Lead, or team leads) and incorporate feedback
5. **Merge & communicate**: Once approved, the change is merged and communicated in the next team update

## Questions or Feedback?

If you have questions about any of these processes or ideas for improvement, please:
- **For urgent issues**: Escalate to your Project Manager
- **For feedback or improvements**: Open an issue with the `documentation` label
- **For clarification**: Ask in team syncs or reach out to your Product Lead
